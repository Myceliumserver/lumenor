Safety & Upgrades

5 Signs Your Home Needs an Electrical Panel Upgrade

Your electrical panel is the heart of your home's electrical system — it controls every circuit and protects against overloads. Most Houston homes built before 1990 have panels that were never designed for today's energy demands. Here's how to know when it's time to upgrade.

1. Your Breakers Keep Tripping

An occasional tripped breaker is normal. But if yours trips frequently — especially under normal household use — your panel is struggling to handle your load. This is a clear sign your system is undersized or failing.

2. You Have a 100-Amp or Smaller Panel

Modern homes need at least 200 amps to safely power HVAC systems, appliances, EV chargers, and home offices. If your panel is rated at 100 amps or less, it's almost certainly undersized for your current needs.

3. Flickering or Dimming Lights

Lights that flicker or dim when you run appliances — especially large ones like air conditioners or washing machines — indicate voltage fluctuations caused by an overloaded or aging panel.

4. You Smell Burning or See Scorch Marks

Any burning smell, discoloration, or scorch marks near your panel is a serious safety emergency. Turn off the main breaker and call a licensed electrician immediately. This can indicate arcing, which is a leading cause of electrical fires.

5. Your Panel Has Recalled Brands

Certain panel brands — including Federal Pacific Electric (FPE) and Zinsco — have been recalled or flagged due to documented failure rates. If you have either of these, a replacement is strongly recommended regardless of how the panel appears to be functioning.

What Does a Panel Upgrade Cost in Houston?

A 200-amp panel upgrade in the Houston area typically runs $1,500–$3,500 depending on the scope of work, permits required, and whether the meter base needs updating. Lumenor provides free on-site estimates so you know exactly what's involved before committing.

EV & Clean Energy

EV Charger Installation: What Houston Homeowners Need to Know

Owning an electric vehicle is becoming increasingly common across Houston. If you're relying on a standard 120V outlet to charge at home, you're leaving a lot of range and convenience on the table. Here's everything you need to know about upgrading to a Level 2 home charger.

Level 1 vs Level 2: What's the Difference?

Level 1 charging uses a standard 120V outlet and adds roughly 4–5 miles of range per hour. Level 2 charging uses a dedicated 240V circuit and delivers 20–30 miles of range per hour — meaning most EVs charge fully overnight. For daily drivers in Houston, Level 2 is the practical standard.

What Does Installation Involve?

A typical Level 2 installation involves: (1) assessing your existing electrical panel to confirm it has available capacity for a 40–50 amp dedicated circuit, (2) running the appropriate wiring from your panel to your garage or parking area, (3) mounting the EVSE (charger unit), and (4) obtaining the required city permit. Most installations are completed in a single day.

Do I Need a Panel Upgrade First?

Not always. Many modern Houston homes already have 200-amp service with room to add a 40–50 amp circuit. If your panel is older or at capacity, a panel upgrade may be recommended. Lumenor assesses this during the free estimate and will never recommend work you don't need.

Which Charger Brands Do You Install?

We install all major brands including ChargePoint, JuiceBox, Tesla Wall Connector, Grizzl-E, and Emporia. We can also install a hardwired outlet if you prefer charger flexibility. We'll recommend the best option for your vehicle and usage pattern.

What Does It Cost in Houston?

Most Level 2 EV charger installations in Houston run $400–$900 for labor and materials (not including the charger itself), depending on the distance from your panel to the installation point and whether any panel work is needed. Charger units typically cost $300–$800 separately.

Storm Preparedness

How to Choose the Right Generator for Your Houston Home

After Hurricane Harvey in 2017 and the February 2021 winter freeze, Houston homeowners learned firsthand how critical backup power is. A whole home standby generator turns on automatically — within seconds of a grid outage — and runs on natural gas or propane so you never have to buy fuel or manually start anything.

Portable Generator vs. Standby Generator

Portable generators are cheaper upfront but require manual setup, fuel storage, and produce carbon monoxide outdoors. They power only a few appliances at a time. Standby generators are permanently installed, kick on automatically, and can power your entire home including HVAC — which in a Houston summer is not optional. For most homeowners, standby is the right long-term investment.

How Much Power Do You Need?

Generator sizing is measured in kilowatts (kW). Here's a general guide for Houston homes: 10–14 kW covers essential circuits (lights, fridge, outlets, small AC unit). 16–20 kW covers most of the home including central AC. 22–26 kW covers a full home with multiple AC units, electric appliances, and an EV charger. We perform a load calculation during your free estimate to recommend the right size.

Natural Gas vs. Propane

If you have natural gas service at your home (most Houston addresses do), a natural gas generator is the most convenient choice — it connects directly to your gas line with no refueling. Propane requires an on-site tank but is available everywhere and can store indefinitely. During the 2021 freeze, natural gas delivery did experience interruptions in some areas, so some homeowners in flood-prone or remote areas prefer propane for resilience.

What Brands Do You Install?

Lumenor installs Generac, Kohler, and Briggs & Stratton standby generators. Generac is the most common brand in Houston with the widest service network. All installations include a transfer switch, load assessment, utility coordination, and full city permitting.

What Does Installation Cost in Houston?

A complete standby generator installation in Houston typically runs $5,000–$12,000 depending on generator size, gas line proximity, and whether an electrical panel upgrade is needed. Generac's popular 22kW unit fully installed runs approximately $8,000–$10,000 in the Houston area. Financing options are available.
